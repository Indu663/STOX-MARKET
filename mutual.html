What is a Mutual Fund?

A mutual fund is a financial vehicle comprised of a pool of money collected from many investors to invest in securities like stocks, bonds, money market instruments, and other assets.

Managed by professional money managers, mutual funds aim to produce capital gains or income for the fund's investors.

Types of Mutual Funds

Equity Funds

Invest in stocks, offering higher risk and potentially higher returns.

Debt Funds

Invest in bonds or other debt securities, generally safer with lower returns.

Balanced Funds

Combine equity and debt, balancing risk and return.

Money Market Funds

Invest in short-term, high-quality investments, focusing on liquidity and safety.

Benefits of Investing in Mutual Funds

  • Diversification: Reduce risk by investing in a wide range of securities.
  • Professional Management: Managed by experienced fund managers.
  • Liquidity: Easily bought or sold at current NAV.
  • Affordability: Accessible to most people, even with a small initial investment.
